Internet Society (ISOC) Research — Web Development Course

a Why was the Internet Society created?

The Internet Society (ISOC) was founded in 1992 by internet pioneers Vint Cerf, Bob Kahn, and Lyman Chapin. It was created as a professional, non-governmental organization to facilitate, support, and promote the evolution and growth of the Internet as a global research and communications infrastructure.

Two key motivations at founding: (1) preserving and archiving Internet documentation and standards, and (2) expanding global awareness of the Internet at a time when most people had never heard of it.

Today, ISOC's mission remains to promote the open development, evolution, and use of the Internet for the benefit of all people worldwide. It supports open standards, digital access, Internet security, and policy advocacy.

b Closest Local Chapter

Since I am based in Mersin, Turkey, the closest ISOC chapter is the Internet Society Turkey Chapter (ISOC-TR), also known as İnternet Derneği.

🌐 Website: http://isoc.org.tr

One activity provided by the Turkey Chapter is publishing an Internet Terminology Dictionary (İnternet Terimleri Sözlüğü) in Turkish, helping local communities better understand internet concepts and standards in their native language. The chapter also participates in Internet governance events and educational initiatives across Turkey.

c How to Join ISOC & My Recommendation

Joining the Internet Society is straightforward: visit isoc.org/become-a-member, fill out the online application form, and select a local chapter (e.g., Turkey Chapter). You will receive a confirmation email with your membership details.

💰 Cost: Free — individual membership has no fees. Anyone, anywhere can join.

My recommendation: Yes, I would encourage a beginning web developer to join ISOC. Even as a newcomer, membership gives you access to webinars, e-learning resources, global networking opportunities, and the ability to stay informed about internet standards and policy — all of which are directly relevant to a career in web development. Since it costs nothing, there is no reason not to join.
